Basque Sports

All of these sports have their origin in rural labor activities. They have been transformed into sports based on the competition between different people to see who has the greatest skill in a given activity. The majority of them require great physical strength and endurance.

  • Harrijasotzea

    Harrijasotzea or harrijasoketa is the name given to stone-lifting, where two players compete to lift a greater number of stones of different shapes, sizes and weights.

  • Aizkolaritza

    Possibly the hardest and at the same time the most spectacular of all the popular sports is the aizkora or axe cutting of logs, which consists of cutting a given log in the shortest possible time.

  • Zesta-punta

    This is a form of Basque pelota played with a wicker basket. In the cesta-punta, the ball is picked up with the basket, and thrown towards the fronton.

  • Euskal pilota

    It generally requires the participation of at least two players or two pairs, who generally take turns hitting a ball against a wall called "fronton" until a goal is scored.

  • Txingak

    It consists of carrying a weight or txinga in each hand for as long as possible along a round trip within a track of usually 28 meters called square or iltze.

  • Sokatira

    Sokatira is a sport that is practiced in the Basque Country and in many other places around the world. It consists of pulling a rope and taking the rival team to your field.
